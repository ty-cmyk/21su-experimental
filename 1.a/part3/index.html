How much CO2 does your data produce?

On average, every human created at least 1.7 MB of data per second in 2020.

For every 1.7MB of data 0.0051kg of CO2 is generated. Which amounts to 440.64kg a day. For comparison the average vehicle in the US generates 9.242kg of CO2 a day.

According to the American Council for an Energy-Efficient Economy it takes 5.12 kWh of electricity per gigabyte of transferred data. And according to the Department of Energy the average US power plant expends 600 grams of carbon dioxide for every kWh generated. That means that transferring 1GB of data produces 3kg of CO2.

These numbers only take into account the CO2 produced by the average electrical grid based on power consumption. Renewable energies such as wind or solar would reduce these numbers drastically.
